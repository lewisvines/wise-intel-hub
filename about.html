01 · Purpose

What the WiSE Intel Hub is and is not

The WiSE Intel Hub is a continuously updated competitive and regulatory intelligence platform built specifically for Sage’s European accountant market, covering France, Spain, Germany and Portugal. It surfaces signals across six categories: competitive moves, regulatory developments, AI and product changes, hiring patterns, pricing, and brand positioning.

It is designed as a decision-support tool, not a replacement for primary research or analyst-grade deep dives. Its purpose is to ensure the PMM, Sales, Marketing and Customer Success teams are never caught off guard by a competitor move, a mandate update that shifts commercial urgency, or a hiring pattern that signals where a rival is investing next.

“The accountant market moves fast. By the time a signal becomes common knowledge, the commercial window has usually closed. WiSE is designed to surface the signal while there is still time to act on it.”

Each signal includes four components: a factual summary, the specific source it was drawn from, the date of the intelligence, and a strategic implication written for Sage’s commercial context. The implication is where editorial judgement is applied — the facts are sourced, the framing is ours.

02 · Source Coverage

Signals are drawn from a structured set of source categories, each weighted by reliability and relevance to the European SMB accounting market. No signal is published without an identifiable, named source. The source is always shown on the card.

03 · Methodology

How signals are identified and validated

Signals go through a structured five-stage process before appearing in the hub. The process is designed to eliminate noise, prevent false positives, and ensure every signal that reaches a user has been checked against one question: does this change what we should do or say?

01
Automated twice-daily scan across 40+ sources

A scheduled process runs at 06:00 and 13:00 UTC on weekdays, searching across the full source library using structured queries per competitor, market and category. Hiring signals are scanned daily. Only content with an identifiable publisher and date is retained.

02
Relevance filter — Europe accountant market only

Results are filtered against defined relevance criteria: does this affect the French, Spanish, German or Portuguese SMB accounting market, the accountant channel, or Sage’s competitive position? Global news with no European accountant implication is discarded.

03
Source verification and dating

Each signal is traced to its primary source. Secondary sources are only used when they cite a primary source. The date of the original publication is used — not the date it was discovered. This prevents stale intelligence being presented as new.

04
Strategic implication — written for Sage’s context

Every signal includes a “Strategic implication” written from Sage’s perspective. This is the only editorial layer. The factual body contains only what is verifiable; the implication applies Sage’s market knowledge to frame the commercial significance.

05
PMM review and publication

Weekly signals are reviewed by the Europe PMM lead before the hub updates. Signals that cannot be verified, are too speculative, or duplicate existing intelligence are removed. The timestamp reflects when PMM review completed, not when the scan ran.

05 · Honest Limitations

What the hub does not capture

Credibility requires honesty about gaps. The WiSE Intel Hub is an excellent early-warning system for publicly observable intelligence. There are categories it structurally cannot cover.

⚠ Known gaps — by design

Private commercial conversations. Deal terms, undisclosed partnerships and private pricing agreements are not observable from public sources. Field sales intelligence is the only channel for this.

Unannounced product roadmaps. What a competitor is building but has not yet announced cannot be surfaced. Hiring patterns provide a probabilistic proxy but cannot confirm direction.

Internal Sage strategy context. The hub surfaces external signals only. Strategic implications must always be read against internal context the PMM team holds.

Breaking news after 13:00 UTC. The second daily scan runs at 13:00 UTC. Events after this point will appear in the following morning’s 06:00 run. For time-critical breaking news, manual update is available on request.

06 · Automation & Governance

How the hub stays current

From April 2026, the WiSE Intel Hub runs on a fully automated twice-daily update cycle, triggered at 06:00 UTC (morning brief) and 13:00 UTC (midday refresh) on weekdays via GitHub Actions. Hiring signals are scanned on every run — not monthly — giving same-day visibility on competitor talent moves.

06:00
Morning briefFull scan across all 6 categories. Overnight news surfaced. Hub updated before working day begins.
13:00
Midday refreshSecond full scan. Captures morning press releases, regulatory announcements and any competitor moves since 06:00.
Weekly
PMM editorial reviewPMM lead reviews and validates high-impact signals. Adds strategic context where needed. Removes unverifiable items.

“The system does not decide what matters. It ensures nothing that matters is missed.”

Version history is maintained in GitHub. Every signal update is timestamped and traceable. If a signal is later found to be incorrect or superseded, the revision is recorded — creating an auditable intelligence trail.

07 · Intended Use

Who uses this and how

The hub is built for four primary audiences within Sage’s European commercial organisation.

Sales teams

Pre-call competitive positioning. “Pennylane is coming to Spain in Q2 — here’s what to say when a Despacho raises it.” Competitive and Pricing signals are the primary utility.

Marketing

Campaign and messaging alignment. If Cegid deploys 100 new salespeople in France, marketing needs to know before the campaign launches. Brand and Hiring signals are the primary utility.

PMM — ELT briefing

Weekly synthesis for leadership. The “If You Read Nothing Else” summary tells the single most important thing that changed this week and why it matters commercially.

Customer Success

Retention preparation. Mandate timeline signals allow CS teams to proactively raise compliance readiness with at-risk accounts before a competitor does it first.
